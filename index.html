Preface

As a parent of two wonderful children, I started writing this book to teach them the value of money. I quickly became enamored with the idea that they should really be learning and practicing entrepreneurship. Entrepreneurship is often mistakenly equated with coding, and many educators seem to be focused on teaching children how to code. While coding is a valuable skill that many parents rightly encourage, I believe that entrepreneurship – the ability to identify problems and create solutions – is even more foundational. Coding is a tool to solve a set of problems. Entrepreneurship is a methodology for solving a bigger set of problems. Entrepreneurship, which encompasses resourcefulness, collaboration, communication, and tool building, is a more powerful skill.

We want our children to be creative, systematic thinkers and resourceful individuals, not just followers of instructions. This book aims to provide a fun and engaging way for kids to learn ceative problem-solving and the value of money. In this interactive fiction story, young readers join our protagonist Daphne in her week-long entrepreneurial adventure. Interactive Fiction books allow the reader to make choices that affect the direction of the story, and thus experience multiple storylines within the same book.

Entrepreneurship typically involves managing resources. In Daphne's adventure, she manages money. To enhance learning, the book allows readers to keep track of Daphne's expenditures and earnings and hence review basic arithmetic. You can hide the piggy bank allowing the reader to calculate Daphne's cash on hand at any point in the story. To aid the arithmetic, you may enable arithmetic hints, or print free play money and track Daphne's earnings or spending in the story. Alternatively, you can choose to keep the piggy bank visible throughout the story.

As a pediatrician and parent, my wife and I are well aware of the dangers of excessive screen time. Therefore, this book is available for e-paper readers and pre-orders are being accepted for a physical copies. The web app and mobile (google play) (ios app store) apps keep distractions to a minimum - The book contains no animations or sounds.
