Preface

As a parent of two wonderful children, I started writing this book to teach them the value of money. I quickly became enamored with the idea that they should really be learning and practicing entrepreneurship. Entrepreneurship is often mistakenly equated with coding, and many educators seem to be focused on teaching children how to code. While coding is a valuable skill that many parents rightly encourage, I believe that entrepreneurship – the ability to identify problems and create solutions – is even more foundational. Coding is a tool to solve a set of problems. Entrepreneurship is a methodology for solving a bigger set of problems. Entrepreneurship, which encompasses resourcefulness, collaboration, communication, and tool building, is a more powerful skill.

We want our children to be creative, systematic thinkers and resourceful individuals. This interactive book aims to provide a fun and engaging way for kids to learn creative problem-solving and the value of money. Readers get to join Daphne in her week-long entrepreneurial adventure, helping Daphne make choices whenever faced with a challenge. Each choice results in a new path in the story, allowing readers to experience multiple storylines within the book.

Like every great entrepreneur carefully managing their resources, Daphne manages her money in a piggy bank. To allow the reader to practice money management and arithmetic along with Daphne, you can choose from several options: Print play money for a hands-on approach to tracking Daphne's earnings and spending (be sure to hide the piggy bank for this option!), enable an arithmetic explainer guide, or simply keep the piggy bank visible.

For parents and readers looking to reduce screen time, this book is available for e-paper readers and a physical book pre-orders are available. The web app and mobile (google play) (ios app store) apps keep distractions to a minimum - The book contains no animations or sounds.
